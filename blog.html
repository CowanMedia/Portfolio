Company website redevelopment

As part of my responsibilities as Marketing Communications Manager at GEX, I am re-developing the company website that met the needs of potential customers, existing customers, and the internal sales team. GEX has some challenges as a small company in a narrow industry that serves large, multinational Fortune 100 companies. Our external image must be highly professional on a limited budget... all while meeting the requirements of customers and the demands of regulatory agencies that govern our customers.

The original site development was done by the very talented Ruben Karel, with nLight Graphics.
www.nLightGraphics.com


Objectives
  • Keep sections that are popular with customers: for example, Customer Satisfaction surveys and sales experiences tell me that the Document Library section is great the way it is.
  • Simplify the navigation on the home page: As the company has grown and added new services, the new items were just tacked as pages and place into the existing navigation.
  • Improve and highlight product "how-to" videos: The Customer Support team has asked to have a video gallery to support customers. The video library is currently buried deep within the document library.
  • Change to https, secure version of http so that site can have forms.
  • Don't spend any money.grade
Navigation solutions

When I originally outlined the site's navigation in 2014, I I used a technique called a card-sorting exercise to better understand how to customers understood our products, and how they intuitively organized the information. I won't go into detail here, but this exercise gets results! I placed product names, services, and other items to appear on our website onto notecards. Then, using a group of customers (reflecting the range of typical users), the customers sorted the cards into conceptual lists.


Example of a card sorting exercise

The results were amazing! It's interesting how differently the customers organized our products and services. Customers' thinking did not reflect how GEX management thought about their own products. The site organization would have made sense to us, but not to our customers. And our annual customer satisfaction surveys support the results - customers consistently love our site and can easily find the information they are looking for. I'm now a big fan of card sorting and doing the work before development!
